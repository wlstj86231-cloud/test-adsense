Shadow People: Figures in the Dark

Shadow people are dark, humanoid figures seen in the periphery of one's vision. They are a global phenomenon, reported by people across all cultures and ages.

ADVERTISEMENT

History and Famous Sightings

Sightings often occur during sleep paralysis or periods of high stress. The most famous 'type' is the 'Hat Man,' a shadow figure wearing a wide-brimmed hat who is often reported as being more malevolent than others.

Scientific and Popular Theories

Scientific explanations include sleep deprivation, hallucinations, or the brain's tendency to find patterns in the dark (pareidolia). Paranormal theories suggest they are interdimensional beings or ghosts.

Cultural Impact and Legacy

Shadow people have become a common trope in horror films and paranormal documentaries, representing the primal fear of what lurks in the corners of our eyes.
