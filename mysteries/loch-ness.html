The Loch Ness Monster: Queen of Cryptids

Deep in the Scottish Highlands lies Loch Ness, a vast and murky body of water that is said to be the home of 'Nessie,' a long-necked aquatic creature that has evaded capture for nearly a century.

ADVERTISEMENT

History and Famous Sightings

The modern legend began in 1933, followed shortly by the famous 'Surgeon's Photograph' in 1934 (which was later revealed as a hoax). Since then, hundreds of sightings and sonar readings have kept the mystery alive.

Scientific and Popular Theories

Could it be a surviving plesiosaur? A giant eel? Or simply logs and boat wakes misidentified by hopeful tourists? Recent DNA surveys of the loch found an abundance of eel DNA but no 'monster' DNA.

Cultural Impact and Legacy

Nessie is a global icon and a major driver of Scottish tourism. She represents the romantic hope that monsters still exist in the unexplored corners of our world.
