The Voynich Manuscript: An Unbreakable Code

The Voynich Manuscript is a 15th-century codex written in an entirely unknown script and language. It is filled with bizarre illustrations of plants that don't exist and celestial diagrams that defy explanation.

ADVERTISEMENT

History and Famous Sightings

Named after the book dealer Wilfrid Voynich who purchased it in 1912, the manuscript has been carbon-dated to the early 1400s. It has been studied by the world's top cryptographers, including Alan Turing's colleagues, yet none have decoded a single word.

Scientific and Popular Theories

Is it a lost language? An elaborate hoax? A coded medical text? Some even suggest it's extraterrestrial in origin. Recent AI attempts have claimed breakthroughs, but the scholarly community remains skeptical.

Cultural Impact and Legacy

The manuscript remains the 'Holy Grail' of cryptography and a symbol of the enduring power of historical enigmas.
